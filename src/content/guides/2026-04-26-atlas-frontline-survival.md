The Atlas can soak an absurd amount of damage **if** you respect its weak spots. Frontal armor is excellent; the lower plate is not.

## Angling fundamentals

- Keep the hull at roughly 30° to incoming fire.
- Hide the lower plate behind terrain whenever possible.
- Never face a slope head-on if you can help it — the shot just goes under your nose.

<Callout type="warning" title="Lower plate exposure">
Even at a perfect 30° angle, a flat lower plate is a free penetration. Pair angling with terrain that hides the bottom hull.
</Callout>

## Sidescraping

When you have a corner, expose only the angled side and pull the lower plate behind cover. Most shells will bounce.

## Knowing when to pull

If you're below 40% HP and your support has rotated, the value of one more trade is almost never worth dying. Reset, reload, push again.

<Callout type="tip">
A heavy that survives to the next engagement does more damage over a match than one that trades 1-for-1 every push.
</Callout>

Atlas isn't unkillable — it's *expensive* to kill. Make them pay for every shot.
